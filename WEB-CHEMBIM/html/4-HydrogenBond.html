5.4 Hydrogen Bond

Hydrogen Bond

Hydrogen bonds are attraction forces between hydrogen atom, H that has bonded with an atom of high electronegativity, such as nitrogen, N, oxygen, O or fluorine, F with nitrogen, N, oxygen, O or fluorine, F in another molecule. For example, water molecule, H2O can form hydrogen bonds among water molecules, H2O.

Hydrogen bonding affects the physical properties of covalent compound such as their melting point and boiling point as well as their solubility in water. Compound that can form hydrogen bond have much higher boiling point than those with Van der Waals force alone. For example, the boiling point of water is much higher than carbon dioxide although the relative molecular mass of water is smaller than carbon dioxide.

Some covalent compound are soluble in water due to the formation of hydrogen bonds between the covalent compound. For example, ethanol, C2H5OH is soluble in water because the -OH group in ethanol forms hydrogen bonds with water molecules.
