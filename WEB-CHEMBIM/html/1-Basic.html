5.1 Basic of Compound Formation

Basic of compound formation

1.

Compound are formed when two or more elements are combined. Most chemicals exist as compounds because compounds are more stable than elements.

2.

In a compound, the atoms are held by chemical bond in a certain ratio.

3.

However, only noble gases exist as monoatomic gases. Noble gases do not form chemical bond because they have duplet or octet electron arrangements which are very stable.

4.

For atoms of others elements from Group 1 to Group 17 which have less than 8 valence electrons. Those atoms will either donate, accept or share electron to achieve stable duplet or octet electron arrangement. How atom can achieve duplet or octet electron arrangement?

5.

Chemical bonds are formed when the atom reached stable electron arrangement by:


•

Transferring electrons from one atom to another, forming an ionic bond.


•

Sharing electrons between two atoms, forming a covalent bond.


6.

There are two types of chemical bonds, ionic bond and covalent bond.


7.

Chemical bond only involves the valence electrons.
