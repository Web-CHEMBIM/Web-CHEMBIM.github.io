5.6 Metallic Bond

Metallic Bond

Metallic Bond is formed by the electrostatic attraction between the closely packed metal cation and a sea of delocalised electrons in metals.

Electric Conductivity in metallic bond

Metals are good conductor of electricity because the delocalised electrons are free to move from the negative terminal to positive terminal. The delocalised electron system is present in both solid and liquid states, so metals can conduct electricity in both solid and liquid states

2. What is meant by a delocalised electron?
Delocalised electrons mean electron that move freely and are not owned by any atom nor ion.
3. Explain the formation of bond mentioned in (1) in metal
The valence electrons of metal atoms can be easily donated and delocalised in solid state. Positively-charged metal ions are formed. All delocalised valence electron can move freely within the metal structure to form an electron sea. The electrostatic attraction between the electron sea and the positively-charged metal ions form metallic bond.
