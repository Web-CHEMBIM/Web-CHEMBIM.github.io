5.3 Covalent Bonding

Formation of Covalent Bond

The covalent bond is formed when non-metal atoms share their electron to achieve a stable duplet or octet electron arrangement. There are three types of covalent bonds: single bond, double bond and triple bond.

Single Bond

The sharing of one pair of electrons between two atoms forms a covalent single bond in a chlorine molecule Cl2.

Double Bond

The sharing of two pairs of electrons between two atoms forms a covalent double bond in an oxygen molecule, O2

Triple Bond

The sharing of three pairs of electrons to produce a covalent triple bond in a nitrogen molecule, N2

An ionic compound is formed when ions of opposite charges attract one another to form an ionic bond. Metal atoms react with non metal atoms to form ionic bonds producing ionic compound. How do ions of opposite charges attract one another?

Steps to draw the electon arrangement of a covalent compound

STEP 1
Determine the formula of the compund from the number of valence electrons or the electron arrangement.
For example, draw the electron arrangement of the compound formed by the carbon and chlorine.
From the periodic table given, we know that [Proton number: C = 6, Cl = 17]
• Carbon, C have 6 proton number with electron arrangement of 2.4, it needs 4 eletrons to achieve stable octet electron arrangement.
• Chlorine, Cl have 17 proton number with electron arrangement of 2.8.7 it needs 1 electron to achieve stable octet electron arrangement.
•

The formula of compound is CCl4

STEP 2
Position the atoms such that the element in the centre is surrounded by the element with the larger number of atoms.
• Position one carbon, C atom in the centre and four chlorine atoms, Cl around it.
• Draw overlapping circle between carbon atoms and chlorine atoms to represent the valence electron shells.
STEP 3
Draw electron pair shared in the overlapping regions of the electron shells.
• Draw four electrons of carbon as crossed in the overlapping regions, one between each chlorine atom.
• Draw one electron from chlorine as dots in the overlapping region between the chlorine and carbon atoms.
STEP 4
Draw the rest of the unshare electrons of each atom outside the overlapping regions of the electron shells.
• Carbon atom, C has no unshared electron (4 - 4 = 0)
• Chlorine atom, Cl has 6 unshared electrons (7 - 1 = 6)
STEP 5
Draw the inner electron shells filled with electrons for all the atoms. Make sure the inner electron shells do not overlap
• Carbon atom, C has one inner electron shell filled with two electrons.
• Chlorine atom, Cl has two inner electron shells filled with two and eight electrons respectively.
