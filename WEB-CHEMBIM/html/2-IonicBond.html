5.2 Ionic Bond

Formation of positive ion

1.

Formation of positive ion. The metal atom donates valence electron to form a positively-charged ion or we can called it as cation. Sodium atom, Na is used as example to describe the formation of cation.

2.

To achieve a stable electron arrangement, sodium atom, Na needs to donate an electron. The process of donating an electron from the valence shell of sodium atom, Na is much easier compared to accepting seven electrons from another atom.

3.

After donating its valence electron, sodium ion, Na+ achives a stable octet electron arrangement. Sodium ion, Na+ has 11 proton and 10 electrons, thus the charge for a sodium ion, Na is +1.

Formation of negative ion

1.

Formation of negative ion. Non metal atom received electron from a metal atom to form a negatively-charged ion or we can called as anion.

2.

To achieve a stable electron arrangement, fluorine atom, F will accept an electron, The process of accepting one electron to the valence shell of fluorine atom, F is easier compared to donating its seven electrons to another atom.

3.

After receiving one valence electron, fluoride ion, F- achieves a stable octet electron arrangement. Fluoride ion, F- has 9 protons and 10 electrons, so the charge of fluoride ion, F- is -1.
